History of the Name Caleb

The name Caleb has its roots in ancient Hebrew, originating in the Old Testament of the Bible. It is derived from the Hebrew word "Kaleb" which is traditionally interpreted to mean "faithful" or "devotion". Historically, Caleb was one of the twelve spies sent by Moses to explore the land of Canaan.

Meaning and Genealogy

The meaning of Caleb has evolved over time. In a broader context, it is often associated with loyalty and bravery. The genealogy of the name can be traced back to Jewish ancestry, and it has been widely adopted in various cultures and regions, adapting unique nuances in each.
